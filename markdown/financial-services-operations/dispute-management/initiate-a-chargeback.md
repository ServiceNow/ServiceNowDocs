# Initiating a chargeback

You can access the **Chargeback** stage by opening a disputed transaction in the **Processing** stage of the playbook. During the **Chargeback** stage, the dispute is forwarded to the card network. The card network then forwards the dispute to the acquirer. There is a wait period to see if there is a representment.

-   **[Report fraud to a card network](report-fraud-to-card-network.md)**  
Report potential fraud on a transaction to a card network.
-   **[Initiate chargeback and recover funds from merchant](initiate-chargeback-and-recover-funds-from-merchant.md)**  
Initiate a request for the card network issuer to credit the customer.
-   **[Review representment](review-representment.md)**  
Review the representment evidence provided by a merchant during a chargeback request. A representment is the process where a business can prove that a charge that is being questioned is valid and the consumer's charge should not be reversed.
-   **[Provide final credit to customer](provide-final-credit-to-customer.md)**  
If a merchant's representment was not approved as sufficient supporting evidence by the issuing bank, the issuing bank upholds the chargeback.
-   **[Convert provisional credit to final credit](convert-provisional-credit-to-final-credit.md)**  
Convert provisional credit previously issued to a customer to a final credit issuance.
-   **[Reverse provisional credit](reverse-provisional-credit.md)**  
Reverse the temporary credit issued by the bank to the card holder.
-   **[Refund a merchant](refund-merchant.md)**  
A merchant can be refunded if their representment evidence is approved.
