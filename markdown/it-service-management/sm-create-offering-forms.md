# Create offering forms

Create offerings to help break down your services into clear, detailed, and user-focused options. Offerings include different locations, commitments, or other dimensions to meet specific needs or business requirements.

Commitment

</td><td>

For offerings differentiated by availability, service level agreements \(SLAs\), or other performance metrics. Commitments occur when a business commits to providing services and offerings to meet customer needs. Availability measures the service being accessible to the customer during the time promised to them. SLAs track whether a vendor provides a level of service for a defined percentage of time. SLAs define a set amount of time for a task to reach a certain condition, to confirm that incidents are closed or resolved according to the expectations set for customers.

</td><td>

Gold, silver, or bronze.For example, let's say that you have multiple SD-WAN product offerings: Gold, Silver, and Bronze. Your remote catalog contains 20 services that are associated with SD-WAN. The Gold offering enables your customer to have rights to all 20 services. The Silver offering enables your customer to have rights to 15 of the 20 services. The Bronze offering enables your customer to have rights to only 10 of the 20 services. You can package all 20 of those services in a single catalog. The customers for each product offering only have the rights to the services that their product offering entitles them to.

</td></tr><tr><td>

Environment

</td><td>

For offerings differentiated by various environments in the instance hierarchy.Environments refer to instance hierarchy. Each level of the instance hierarchy serves a specific purpose.

</td><td>

Production, non-production, sandbox, test, or development. Non-production instances, like a sandbox environment, provide a safe space to experiment with new ideas.

</td></tr><tr><td>

Location

</td><td>

For offerings impacted by countries, offices, or regions.

</td><td>

APAC, EMEA, NA, IMEA, or other specific locations.Location-based offerings like ride-share applications or roadside assistance depend on exact location. Other examples include store locators, weather reports, delivery tracking, and mitigating credit card fraud.
