# Exploring Service Modeler

Learn about Service Modeler benefits to manage all of your solutions in one place. Solutions in the context of Service Modeler are business services, technology management services, service instances, and offerings.

## Service Modeler Overview

Create and maintain definitions of your services and offerings through a guided experience. The interview-style conversation prompts you with questions every step of the way, from defining your service and creating offerings to validating, getting approvals, and finally, publishing your service.

Include outcomes here.

## Interactive guided experience

![Service Modeler landing page](../image/sd-landing-page.png)

To access Service Modeler, log in to your provided ServiceNow instance and then select **Workspaces &gt; Digital Portfolio Management**. From the DPM homepage, select the Service Modeler icon \(\).

The landing page includes the following main areas:

-   Potential service opportunities suggested by Now Assist
-   An overview of your draft and published services
-   List of services along with their offerings, owner, phase, state, service type, and approval status

From the landing page, create a service by selecting one of the two options:

-   **Create service**
-   **Create with AI**

## Create service

Answer a series of questions to create a service. You may apply AI-predicted answers to some questions to help you throughout the process. Review and edit AI-predicted answers before moving forward. For more information on how to create a service, see [Create a service in Service Modeler](../task/sm-create-service.md).

## Data processing and collection

This application requires data to be transferred from ServiceNow customers' individual instances to a centralized ServiceNow environment, which may be located in a different region from the one where your instance is, and potentially to a third-party cloud provider, such as Microsoft Azure. This data is handled per ServiceNow's internal policies and procedures, including policies available through [CORE Compliance Portal](https://support.servicenow.com/kb?id=kb_article_view&sysparm_article=KB0564067).

ServiceNow collects and uses the inputs, outputs, and edits to outputs of this application to develop and improve ServiceNow technologies including ServiceNow models and AI products. In addition, this application will collect change records and incident data. Customers can opt out of future data collection at any time, as described in the [Now Assist Opt-Out page](https://www.servicenow.com/docs/access?context=opt-out-of-data-sharing-for-now-assist&version=australia&pubname=australia-intelligent-experiences&ft:locale=en-US).![The Now Assist panel provides details about the potential service opportunity that you selected.](../image/sd-create-with-ai.png)

View the potential service opportunities suggested by Now Assist and select **Create with AI** to open the Now Assist panel. For more detailed information about creating a service using Now Assist, see [Create a service with Now Assist in Service Modeler](../task/sm-create-service-now-assist.md).

After selecting **Create with AI**, the Now Assist panel opens up with details about the suggested service including the service name, service type, description, and service owner. Review the suggestions. For information about the justification for creating the service and its potential impact, select **Show**.

![Detailed reasoning behind the suggestions by Now Assist include key data and analysis.](../image/sd-now-assist-detail-reasoning.png)

The detailed reasoning section shows key data, observations, and analysis to justify the creation of the service suggested by Now Assist. Select the links to view various records. After reviewing the information, select **Start creation journey** to create the service using the details suggested by Now Assist. You can cancel out of creating a service with AI and return to the landing page.

## Service Modeler users

|Users|Description|
|-----|-----------|
|Service architects|Responsible for modeling and defining services and offerings.|
|Service owners|Accountable for the end-to-end lifecycle of services and offerings.|
|Service managers|Responsible for the day-to-day management of the services and offerings.|

|Role|Description|
|----|-----------|
|Service admin|The service admin role can create services and edit any services, even services that they didn't create.|
|Service author|The service author role can create services and edit the services that they create.|
|Service viewer|The service editor role can edit services that they own. They can't create services.|
