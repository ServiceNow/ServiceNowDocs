# Business Process Form

A Business process is a method of related structured tasks performed to complete a specific application service.

## Business Process Form Fields

<table id="table_x2x_2hh_px"><thead><tr><th>

Field

</th><th>

Description

</th></tr></thead><tbody><tr><td>

Name

</td><td>

Unique name for the business process.

</td></tr><tr><td>

Asset tag

</td><td>

Inherited CMDB field. This field is typically used for physical assets and is not applicable to business process records. You can leave this field empty when creating or updating a business process.

</td></tr><tr><td>

Assigned to

</td><td>

Person using or responsible for the item.

</td></tr><tr><td>

Category

</td><td>

Category of the business process.

</td></tr><tr><td>

Fault count

</td><td>

Number of faults.

</td></tr><tr><td>

Installed

</td><td>

Date and time when the business application is installed.

</td></tr><tr><td>

Install Status

</td><td>

Operational status of the business process. Use this field to indicate whether the process is active or no longer in use. Common values include:

-   Installed \(active\)
-   In Maintenance \(temporarily suspended\)
-   Retired \(no longer used\)

 Other values may appear due to CMDB inheritance and are not typically relevant for business processes.
